Expert tips & FAQs

Can a windscreen chip be repaired?

Yes — most chips smaller than a $2 coin can be repaired if they're not in the driver's direct line of sight. Repair is faster, cheaper and better for the environment than replacement.

How long does a windscreen replacement take?

Most replacements take 60–90 minutes. We recommend waiting at least one hour before driving to allow the adhesive to set properly.

Do you handle insurance claims?

Absolutely. We work with all major Australian insurers and can often bill them directly. Bring your policy details and we'll take care of the rest.

Is mobile service available in my area?

Mobile service is available across most metro and regional areas. Enter your postcode in the booking form and we'll confirm availability for your location.

What is ADAS recalibration?

Modern vehicles use cameras and sensors mounted to the windscreen for safety features like lane assist. After replacement, these systems must be recalibrated to work correctly.
